Our Science

Peer-reviewed earthquake forecasting methodology with proven results

Revolutionary Earthquake Forecasting

OpenHazards employs two groundbreaking methodologies to deliver the world's most advanced earthquake probability assessments: Pattern Informatics (PI) and Natural Time Weibull (NTW) analysis.

Unlike traditional approaches that rely on historical fault data alone, our methods detect statistically significant changes in seismic patterns across entire regions, enabling more accurate short- to medium-term forecasts.

Core Technologies

Pattern Informatics (PI)

Developed by Prof. John Rundle and colleagues, Pattern Informatics identifies regions where earthquake activity patterns are changing significantly compared to historical baselines.

How it works:

  • Analyzes spatio-temporal seismicity patterns
  • Detects "hot spots" and "cold spots" of activity
  • Identifies precursory pattern changes
  • Updates daily with new seismic data

Natural Time Weibull (NTW)

Pattern Informatics: Technical Deep Dive

Mathematical Foundation

Pattern Informatics uses statistical physics principles to identify regions where seismic activity deviates significantly from historical patterns. The method calculates a "pattern function" that measures changes in earthquake rates across space and time.

Key Components:

  • Spatial Grid: Earth's surface divided into 0.1° × 0.1° cells
  • Time Windows: Analysis of overlapping periods to detect pattern evolution
  • Statistical Significance: Changes evaluated against null hypothesis of random variation
  • Pattern Function: Mathematical measure of seismicity rate changes

Pattern Function:

ΔP(x,t) = |S(x,t₂) - S(x,t₁)| / σ(x)

Where S is the seismicity rate and σ is the standard deviation

Natural Time Weibull: Probability Engine

From Patterns to Probabilities

The Natural Time Weibull method transforms PI pattern maps into actionable probability forecasts. It applies survival analysis concepts from reliability engineering to earthquake occurrence.

Process Steps:

  1. Natural Time Transform: Convert calendar time to "natural time" based on cumulative seismic moment
  2. Weibull Distribution: Fit earthquake occurrence data to Weibull probability model
  3. Conditional Probability: Calculate likelihood given current seismic state
  4. Magnitude Scaling: Apply Gutenberg-Richter relationships for magnitude-specific forecasts

Performance Metrics:

  • Probability gain: 5-10x over uniform random forecast
  • Area skill score: 0.20-0.25 (significant positive skill)
  • Molchan diagram: Consistently above diagonal (better than random)
