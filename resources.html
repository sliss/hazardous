Earthquake Safety Guide

Essential information to protect yourself and your family

Before an Earthquake

  • Create an emergency plan with your family
  • Identify safe spots in each room (under sturdy tables, against interior walls)
  • Secure heavy items that could fall
  • Prepare an emergency kit with supplies for 72 hours
  • Know how to turn off gas, water, and electricity
  • Review your insurance coverage
Download Checklist

During an Earthquake

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On
  • If indoors, stay there - don't run outside
  • Take cover under a sturdy desk or table
  • Stay away from windows, mirrors, and heavy objects
  • If outdoors, move away from buildings and power lines
  • If driving, pull over safely and stay in the vehicle
View Infographic

After an Earthquake

  • Check for injuries and provide first aid
  • Inspect your home for damage
  • Turn off utilities if you suspect damage
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts
  • Expect aftershocks and be prepared
  • Document damage for insurance claims

Frequently Asked Questions

About Our Forecasts

How accurate are OpenHazards earthquake forecasts?

Our forecasts have demonstrated 80-85% spatial accuracy in back-testing, successfully identifying 16 of 18 major California earthquakes from 2002-2005. However, earthquake forecasting remains probabilistic—we provide likelihood estimates, not definitive predictions.

How often are forecasts updated?

Our system updates daily at 21:30 PT, incorporating the latest seismic data from USGS and international monitoring networks. This ensures our probability assessments reflect the most current seismic conditions.

What's the difference between forecasting and prediction?

Earthquake prediction would specify exact time, location, and magnitude—which is not currently possible. Forecasting provides probability estimates for earthquakes within regions and time windows, similar to weather forecasting.

Using Our Tools

Is the Hazard Viewer really free?

Yes, our Hazard Viewer is completely free for public use. We believe earthquake safety information should be accessible to everyone. Enterprise clients requiring API access or custom analytics should contact us for pricing.

How do I interpret probability percentages?

A 10% probability of a M5+ earthquake in 1 year means there's a 1-in-10 chance of such an event. For context, this is significantly elevated compared to background rates. Always consider these probabilities alongside local building codes and preparedness measures.

Can I use your data for research?

Yes, academic researchers can use our publicly available data. Please cite our published papers and website. For bulk data access or collaboration opportunities, contact our science team.

API Documentation

Integrate earthquake forecasting into your applications

OpenHazards API v2.0

Our RESTful API provides programmatic access to earthquake probability forecasts, historical data, and risk assessments. Perfect for insurance platforms, emergency management systems, and research applications.

Key Features:

  • Real-time probability queries for any location
  • Bulk processing for multiple coordinates
  • Historical earthquake data access
  • WebSocket support for live updates
  • Multiple response formats (JSON, XML, CSV)

Quick Start Example

GET /api/v2/forecast
  ?lat=37.7749
  &lon=-122.4194
  &magnitude=5.0
  &timeframe=1year

Response:
{
  "location": {
    "latitude": 37.7749,
    "longitude": -122.4194
  },
  "forecast": {
    "probability": 0.082,
    "magnitude_threshold": 5.0,
    "timeframe_days": 365,
    "last_updated": "2025-05-15T21:30:00Z"
  }
}
